What we study

Research

GeoL's research addresses the geomechanical and geotechnical challenges of deep underground mining — from rock mass characterization to numerical modeling, seismicity analytics, and mine design at depth.

Research areas
Where we focus

Three core lines of research sustain our applied work in deep mining geomechanics.

01

Geotechnical characterization

Geotechnical units, structural geology, rock mass quality, and in-situ stress fields in underground operations such as El Teniente and Chuquicamata.

02

Deep underground mining

Cavity interaction, hydraulic fracturing for seismicity management, caving behavior, fragmentation, and stability challenges as mines grow deeper.

03

Innovation and electrification

Unit-process automation, advanced seismic analytics, and technology applied to mine design, electrification, and production continuity.

Publications
